Project Nimbus

Weather intelligence---the use short, medium, and long-term forecasts for improved decision-making in critical sectors including agriculture and engery---is increasingly vital in the face of unprecidented changes in weather and climate. However, the availablity of localized, high-quality forecasts is not equitable around the world; regions that are unable to make large investments in specialized measurment equitment or computational resources suffer from disparities in forecast quality, actionablity, and usablity. Project Nimubs aims to address global inequity in weather intelligence. Leveraging strong partnerships in Kenya, and funded by the Gates Foundation, Project Nimbus will provide innovative technical solutions to improve weather intelligence in East Africa, with a focus on providing actionable insights for small holder farmers.

The Power EQuality project aims to highlight the global disparity of access to high-quality electricity globally and propose solutions to improve power quality by coalescing a community around the issue, building open power-quality monitoring solutions, and working with specific utilities and policy-makers to improve power quality globally.
